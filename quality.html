Home / Quality Control

Four checkpoints stand between every raw shell and your container.

Consistency is a process, not a promise. Here is exactly how we verify it, batch by batch.

01

Raw Material Screening

Incoming coconut shells are inspected for maturity, moisture content and contamination before entering the carbonization queue. Shells that don't meet density standards are rejected at intake.

Moisture CheckDensity ScreeningVisual Sorting
02

In-Process Carbonization Monitoring

Kiln temperature and burn duration are logged for every batch to ensure uniform carbon conversion — the single biggest factor in consistent calorific value across shipments.

Kiln Temp LoggingBatch Tracking
03

Laboratory Testing

Finished briquettes are tested in-house for ash content, fixed carbon, calorific value, moisture and burning time — with results logged against each production batch number for full traceability.

Ash ContentCalorific ValueBurn Time Trials
04

Pre-Shipment Inspection

Before loading, a final sample is drawn from the packed container. Buyers may request independent SGS or Bureau Veritas inspection at this stage for full assurance.
